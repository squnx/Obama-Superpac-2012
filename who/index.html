We are a Presidential Advertising Committee.

Unlike Political Action Committees, who spend millions of dollars to influence the outcome of public elections, the Obama SuperPac is made up of an elite group of advertising warriors. Spearheaded by Harry Webber, the creator of some of the most memorable advertising campaigns of all time, we are veterans on Madison Avenue who have come together to do what we do best. Together, we are dedicated to communicating the value of the President's second term in office to the American public at large in ways that are impactful, creative and memorable.

But let's be clear: the President is our product, not our client. We are an independent group. Mr. Obama has nothing to say about how we present his accomplishments to the electorate. In fact, we are proud to say that to date no political candidate or candidate's organization has authorized our messages.

The Obama SuperPac has operatives in Chicago, New York, Los Angeles, St. Louis, Dallas, and Atlanta. But unlike the other Super PACs, we don't have millions and millions of dollars. The Obama SuperPac operates on spare change and lunch money. We spend every last dime creating advertising for a single candidate: the President of the United States.

Our network is growing.

Thanks to the power of the internet, we operate lean and mean. By posting our work on social networks like Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, Pinterest and LinkedIn, these campaigns can then be shared among millions and millions of friends, fans and subscribers.

The Obama SuperPac is a network of networks creating and amplifying great advertising for a single goal: the re-election of the President. Every time you share one of our ads with someone you care about, our network grows larger and stronger.

Thank you. And keep up the good work.

We are funded by people just like you.

Well, almost. The Obama SuperPac is the ultimate result of a donation from a billionaire in Texas who gave us $50,000 to make a difference in the election. But we've also been receiving donations from people just like you. People who care about our country. And people who want to do something about the America that will exist for their children in the future.

And thanks to you, we're off to a great start. To date, our awareness campaign includes the creation of 16 video commercials, the infiltration of 5 social networking platforms and the creation of 2 informational websites and a blog. There is still much to be done and little time remaining before the November 6th election. The Obama SuperPac can use your help. Won't you join us?
